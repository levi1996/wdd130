Arkansas River

The Arkansas River is a major tributary of the Mississippi River. It generally flows to the east and southeast as it traverses the U.S. states of Colorado, Kansas, Oklahoma, and Arkansas. Famed for its dramatic rapids and canyons, the Colorado is one of the most desirable whitewater rivers in the United States, and its Grand Canyon section—run by more than 22,000 people annually has been called the "granddaddy of rafting trips".

Colorado River

The Colorado River (Spanish: Río Colorado) is one of the principal rivers (along with the Rio Grande) in the Southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The 1,450-mile-long (2,330 km) river drains an expansive, arid watershed that encompasses parts of seven U.S. states and two Mexican states.

The Middle Fork of the Salmon River

The Middle Fork of the Salmon River is a 104-mile-long (167 km) river in central Idaho in the northwestern United States.The middle fork is an exceptionally popular and difficult whitewater rafting and kayaking destination. Given Federal protection in 1980, the wilderness area it lies within is part of the largest roadless tract left in the lower 48 states.
